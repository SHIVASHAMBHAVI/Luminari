About Hampi

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an ancient treasure trove nestled in the heart of Karnataka, India. Once the thriving capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century, it is now a stunning labyrinth of awe-inspiring ruins, temples, and royal enclosures. The landscape of Hampi is dotted with colossal boulders, ancient market streets, and towering pillars, all echoing with stories of glory and grandeur. Highlights like the Virupaksha Temple, the iconic Stone Chariot, and the tranquil Lotus Mahal paint a vivid picture of a once-flourishing civilization. The grandiose Vittala Temple complex, with its musical pillars, and the breathtaking views from Hemakuta Hill add to the allure of this historic marvel.
